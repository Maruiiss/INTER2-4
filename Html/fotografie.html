Photography

Introduction

In the field of photography, a level F grade can provide students with valuable skills and knowledge to become a professional photographer.

Course Overview

Year 1

Learn how to work with different types of cameras, both in the studio and on location. Understand how to set up the camera to capture the image you have in mind. Discover your unique photographic style. Learn to work with the most important software and find out how you can make a living from photography.

Year 2

Deepen your knowledge of the subject and start practicing. Search for the direction in photography where you can earn money in the future. Prepare for the stage that begins halfway through the second year. Work at a company or independent photographer to gain practical experience. Complete some vocational exams during the first year.

Year 3

The third year begins with the second part of your internship. During the last period of the third year, you will graduate. End, along with your fellow students, with a spectacular exhibition where you can shine with your photos!

After Graduation

After your photography degree, you can work in the creative industry, at photography studios, magazines, newspapers, or other media. You can also consider further education at the university, such as a degree in fine arts. If you want to start your own business, consider the GLRXTRA program, an additional year at the GLR that focuses on entrepreneurship and starting your own business.
